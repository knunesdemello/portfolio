Project Brief:

Professor Anvari gave us the task of bringing to light issues with existing Apps in order to create a more wholesome experience to users. I chose to focus on one of the top problems a traveler has while away from home, at a country that employs a different conversion system.
Provide essential information offline, travelers always feel secure, while also aiming for a simple, intuitive, design that allows them to find out current Tax-rates for example, which vary from city to city, and add to the accuracy of the total of a currency conversion. This issue also covers individuals who have moved to the US, and are not yet used to the Imperial system of measurement.

Personas:

  • Three personas were created as a result of 6 interviews with individuals of various ages, backgrounds, professions, and residents of either the US and Brazil. These are the key Observations extracted from the interviews:

    1. 1. Most travelers still use Google for metric and currency conversions, which presents a problem for when internet connection is lacking. It is also interesting to see that cellphones currently have Apps such as calculator, stocks, and maps, that may work offline, but no built in metric converters.

    2. 2. All interviewees agreed that keeping up with conversions can take a toll on the enjoyment of their trips, and all showed interest on an app that is simple to use.

    3. 3. In terms of affordability, most of them suggested either a one time fee, or yearly subscription. Most importantly, NO ADS.

    4. 4. This App would be profitable as it is not only targeted towards travelers, traveling professionals, or those living abroad but also from an educational stand point.

    5. 5. The idea for a Conversion App also resonated well with my audience, as they find it frustrating to keep up with exchange rates that vary from region to region and country to country.

    6. 6. After interviewing 6 candidates, I decided to break the target up into niches. Travelers and students to begin with.
